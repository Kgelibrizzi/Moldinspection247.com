Removing Mold

In some cases, removing mold is very simple. In other cases, it can be quite challenging and even dangerous. We help you determine what category your situation falls under and what level of risk may apply in your case.

Common mold situations like shower mold, small surface spotting on walls, or mild odor in closets often don’t require laboratory testing or professional remediation — but larger visible growth or mold resulting from a plumbing leak, storm event, or hidden water intrusion may.

When to Involve a Professional

When the mold is widespread, recurring, or related to a known moisture event, or if anyone in the household has known respiratory or immune conditions, we recommend a professional inspection. We follow ANSI/IICRC S520 guidelines and can assist in recommending licensed remediation contractors if needed.

We also help you determine whether mold testing is useful or not. Some cases clearly don’t need testing. In others, air sampling or clearance testing may be critical for real estate, insurance, or health-related decisions.

Our Process

  • Site visit with inspection and moisture screening
  • Digital photos and documentation
  • Clear explanation of findings and risk level
  • Recommendations based on best practices and Florida statute
  • Testing only if justified (we explain why or why not)
