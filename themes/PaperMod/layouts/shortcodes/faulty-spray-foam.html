Faulty Spray Foam Insulation

Spray foam insulation is praised for its energy efficiency and air-sealing benefits. But when installed incorrectly, it can become a hidden source of indoor air quality (IAQ) and moisture issues — especially in Florida’s humid climate.

Signs of faulty spray foam may include:

  • Persistent chemical odors or musty smells
  • Peeling paint, rusting metal, or condensation in attics or wall cavities
  • Mold growth in areas where moisture should be controlled
  • Hot rooms or unexpected utility spikes (due to blocked ventilation)

These issues often stem from poor installation practices — such as overapplication, improper mixing, or failure to maintain airflow in critical areas like attics or rooflines.
